Basti Therapy
The MOST Important for Kidney Healing
What is it?

Basti (Enema Therapy) is considered the "half-treatment" in Ayurveda because it cleanses the Vata dosha, which is the primary energy force behind all movement and elimination in the body. For kidney patients, medicated oil or decoction enemas help remove accumulated toxins (Ama) and balance the body's fluid regulation.

Steps to Perform
  • Consult an Ayurvedic practitioner for the specific medicated oil or decoction suitable for your condition.
  • Usually performed in the morning on an empty stomach or evening.
  • Lie on your left side with the right leg folded.
  • Administer the medicated liquid gently through the rectum.
  • Retain the fluid for the prescribed duration (usually 30-45 minutes) before evacuation.
Benefits
Flushes out toxins (urea/creatinine)
Balances Vata dosha
Improves kidney function
Reduces dryness in the body
Note: This therapy should strictly be performed under the guidance of a qualified Ayurvedic doctor.
Abhyanga
Self Oil Massage
What is it?

Abhyanga is the practice of massaging the body with warm, medicated oils. It nourishes the tissues, penetrates deep into the cells, and helps release physical and mental toxins.

Steps to Perform
  • Warm up organic sesame oil or medicated Ayurvedic oil (like Mahanarayan oil).
  • Apply the warm oil to your entire body, starting from the scalp to the soles of your feet.
  • Use long strokes on limbs and circular motions on joints.
  • Massage gently for 15-20 minutes.
  • Let the oil absorb for another 10-15 minutes before taking a warm bath.
Benefits
Improves circulation
Reduces stress & fatigue
Strengthens the body
Promotes better sleep
Swedana
Herbal Steam at Home
What is it?

Swedana is a steam therapy that induces sweating. It helps liquefy toxins deep in the tissues, making it easier for the body to expel them. It is often done after Abhyanga.

Steps to Perform
  • After your oil massage, sit in a steam box or a steam room if available.
  • At home: Boil water with herbs (like ginger or eucalyptus) in a large pot.
  • Sit on a chair with a blanket covering you and the pot (be careful not to burn yourself).
  • Allow the steam to envelop your body (excluding the head) for 10-15 minutes.
  • Wipe off the sweat and take a warm shower.
Benefits
Opens pores & releases toxins
Relieves stiffness
Improves circulation
Reduces heaviness
Castor Oil Hot Pack
Targeted Kidney Therapy
What is it?

This involves applying a pack soaked in warm castor oil over the kidney area (lower back). Castor oil has deep penetrating and anti-inflammatory properties that stimulate lymphatic drainage and kidney function.

Steps to Perform
  • Soak a piece of flannel or cotton cloth in warm castor oil.
  • Place the cloth on your lower back (over the kidney region).
  • Cover it with plastic wrap to prevent staining.
  • Place a hot water bottle or heating pad over it.
  • Relax for 30-45 minutes.
Benefits
Stimulates kidney function
Reduces inflammation
Relieves lower back pain
Detoxifies the area
Virechana
Therapeutic Purgation
What is it?

Virechana is a controlled process of cleansing the digestive tract, specifically the small intestine and liver, through the use of laxative herbs. It eliminates excess Pitta dosha and toxins.

Steps to Perform
  • Requires preparation (Snehana and Swedana) for a few days prior.
  • On the day of therapy, specific herbal laxatives (like castor oil or herbal powders) are administered in the morning.
  • This induces multiple bowel movements to flush out toxins.
  • Follow a strict diet (Samsarjana Krama) afterwards to restore digestive fire.
Benefits
Cleanses liver & blood
Reduces body heat (Pitta)
Improves metabolism
Clears skin issues
Note: Virechana is a strong detoxification procedure and must ONLY be done under the supervision of an Ayurvedic physician.
